It's 2024 and I'm starting a blog for the first time. When the tech circles are all about the latest and greatest LLM models, and the internet is full of AI-generated text. So why now? What's even the point of writing anything these days?

## The Quantified Self

The pandemic changed a lot, mostly for the better. All of a sudden, I became all about tracking everything. It started with tracking my daily temperature to provide some temporary relief to all the covid-related anxiety. Then it was tracking weight to get in a better shape. That lead to a realization that tracking my progress motivated me to keep going even after losing interest. It became a habit and a way to push myself further than I ever have.

By the time all the lockdowns were over, I was tracking temperatue, blood pressure, weight, sleep, screen time, workouts, and meditations. I had become [The Quantified Self](https://www.economist.com/technology-quarterly/2022-05-07).

## Tracking

My learning methodology has always been very ad-hoc. I'll pick up a book whenever I felt like I had to learn something about a topic and just keep digging in until my interest waned. Ever since university days, I never put any effort into tracking what I was learning.

This blog is my way of expanding my new-formed tracking habit from physical activities and metrics and into learning ideas and skills. If I can keep a track of what I'm learning and building, I can push myself even further in the direction I want.

## Accountability

The goal of this blog is to make sure I keep myself accountable. If I commit to learning something, this place will help me figure out how far I've gotten along in the process, and if it's worth digging in any deeper. If I abandon something due to whatever reasons, this place will help me stay accountable and try to get back into it.

## Fun

So what if all the words go into the ether, only to be picked up by text crawler? At least I helped improve a model.

So what if nobody will ever read it? At least I put some thought into it, and that alone is worth the effort.

Writing is thinking, and thinking is fun. And I'm just having some fun here!
